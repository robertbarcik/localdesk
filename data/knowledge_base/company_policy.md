# IT Usage Policy — LocalDesk IT Services

**Policy Owner:** Chief Information Security Officer
**Last Updated:** December 2025
**Applies to:** All employees, contractors, and authorized third parties

---

## 1. Acceptable Use

### 1.1 General Principles
All IT resources provided by the company — including hardware, software, network access, email, and cloud services — are company property intended for business purposes. Limited personal use is permitted provided it does not interfere with work duties, consume excessive bandwidth, or violate any section of this policy.

### 1.2 Prohibited Activities
The following activities are strictly prohibited on company IT resources:
- Accessing, downloading, or distributing illegal, offensive, or discriminatory content
- Installing unauthorized software or circumventing security controls
- Using company resources for cryptocurrency mining or personal commercial ventures
- Sharing credentials or authentication tokens with unauthorized individuals
- Connecting unauthorized devices to the corporate network without IT approval
- Attempting to access systems, data, or accounts beyond your authorized scope
- Using company email for mass unsolicited communications

---

## 2. Bring Your Own Device (BYOD)

### 2.1 Registration and Approval
Personal devices used for work purposes must be registered with IT and approved before accessing company resources. Registration is completed through the IT self-service portal or by contacting the service desk.

### 2.2 Requirements for BYOD
- Device must run a supported operating system with current security patches
- Full-disk encryption must be enabled
- Screen lock with PIN/biometric must be configured (maximum 5-minute timeout)
- Company-approved MDM (Mobile Device Management) profile must be installed
- Antivirus/endpoint protection software must be active and up to date

### 2.3 Data Separation
Work data accessed via BYOD must remain within company-managed applications (e.g., managed email client, approved cloud storage). Copying work data to personal storage, apps, or accounts is prohibited.

### 2.4 Lost or Stolen Devices
Report lost or stolen BYOD devices to IT within 2 hours. IT reserves the right to remotely wipe company data from the device.

---

## 3. Data Classification

### 3.1 Classification Levels
All company data must be handled according to its classification:

- **Public:** Information approved for external distribution (marketing materials, published reports). No special handling required.
- **Internal:** General business information not intended for external audiences. Store on company systems, do not share externally without manager approval.
- **Confidential:** Sensitive business data (financial records, HR data, customer contracts, strategic plans). Encrypt in transit and at rest. Access restricted to need-to-know basis.
- **Restricted:** Highly sensitive data (personal data under GDPR, trade secrets, security credentials). Strongest controls: encryption mandatory, access logged, sharing requires CISO approval.

### 3.2 Data Handling
- Never send Confidential or Restricted data via unencrypted email
- Use company-approved file sharing tools for Confidential data
- Restricted data must not be stored on personal devices or removable media
- Printed Confidential/Restricted documents must be collected immediately and shredded when no longer needed

---

## 4. Incident Reporting

### 4.1 Obligation to Report
All employees must report suspected security incidents, data breaches, or policy violations immediately. Reporting should be done through:
1. The IT service desk (preferred — creates an auditable record)
2. Direct communication with your manager or the IT Security team
3. The anonymous reporting hotline (for sensitive matters)

### 4.2 What to Report
- Suspected phishing emails or social engineering attempts
- Unauthorized access to systems or data
- Lost or stolen devices or credentials
- Unusual system behavior or suspected malware
- Accidental disclosure of confidential information
- Physical security concerns (tailgating, unsecured server rooms)

### 4.3 Non-Retaliation
The company maintains a strict non-retaliation policy. No employee will face adverse consequences for reporting a genuine security concern in good faith, even if the report turns out to be a false alarm.

---

## 5. Enforcement

Violations of this policy may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment, and in severe cases, legal proceedings. The IT Security team conducts periodic audits to ensure compliance.
